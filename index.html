About

Mary Wanesia is a jeweler, and Arizona native, and a memeber of the Navajo tribe. She specializes in creatign beautiful contemporary takes on the
traditional adornments worn by Navajo in ceramonies and daily life.

Jewelry in the Americas has an ancient history. Beginning as far back as 8800 BCE, Paleo-Indians in the American Southwest drilled and shaped
multicolored stones and shells into beads and pendants. Olivella shell beads, dating from 6000 BCE, were found in Nevada; bone, antler, and
possibly marine shell beads from 7000 BCE were found in Russel Cave in Alabama; copper jewelry was traded from Lake Superior beginning in
3000 BCE; and stone beads were carved in Poverty Point in Louisiana in 1500 BCE. Learn more.

Mary's jewelry has received recognition, and been featured in the National Museum of the American Indian, The New York Times Style Magazine,
and Sedona Monthly. The collection has been sold across the United States and Europe and by the retail stores of Bergdorf Goodman.
